Starting a sprint

A sprint is a fixed period of time, usually two to four weeks, in which tasks are completed to achieve the product goal.

Before you can start your first sprint there are some preparations that need to be done. The Product Owner needs to make a product backlog (see list of terms). After the product backlog is made the Product Owner and the Scrum team will have a sprint planning meeting to discuss which items they will work on during the sprint. When this is clear the first sprint can start.

During a sprint the Scrum Team will have a short meeting (about 15 minutes) every day at the same time to update each other. Each team member tells the rest of the team what he or she did the previous day and which task are on the schedule for today.

When a sprint is finished the Scrum Team will have a sprint review meeting to show what they have accomplished during the sprint. During a sprint review meeting a live demonstration of the product is given to the Product Owner. After the demonstration the Product Owner decides which task can be marked as done. When not all the desired items are done, they return to the product backlog. After the sprint review meeting a sprint retrospective meeting will take place. In this meeting the Scrum Team reviews their personal process and applies the feedback they had been given before the next sprint starts.
