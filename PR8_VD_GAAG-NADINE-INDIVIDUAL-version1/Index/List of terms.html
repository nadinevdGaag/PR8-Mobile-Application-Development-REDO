List of terms

Product backlog
The product backlog is a list of items that need to be executed to develop the product. On the product backlog you can add changes or extensions that affect your product development. The Product Owner prioritizes the items on the product backlog. After the items are prioritize the Product Owner and the Scrum Team can estimate how much the workload will be and calculate their return of investment.

Sprint backlog
The sprint backlog is a subgroup of the product backlog. Unlike the product backlog the sprint backlog only contains the item or items that can be finished during each sprint.

Burndown chart
A burndown chart is a chart that shows the amount of work that is done every day versus the amount of work that was estimated. The purpose of a burndown chart is to ensure that the project is on the right track and the deadline will be met. See the example below.

Sprint retrospective
After every sprint the Scrum Team reflects on its own process. They provide each other with feedback so they can keep developing themselves and do better during future sprints.

Acceptance testing
Acceptance testing is the process of testing a product to make sure it meets the set acceptance criteria. The acceptance criteria are the criteria a product must meet to satisfy the customer/stakeholder.

Scrum board
The scrum board is the board where everyone can find all the items that are on the sprint backlog. The items on the board are divided in three columns: to do, in progress and done. The most import task hang on top of the scrum board.

Test driven development
Test driven development is a software development technique where you have to make a test that fails before you can write a new test that does not fail.

Time box
A time box is an agreed timeslot in which a task must be done.

Team velocity
Team velocity is the amount of work a team can handle in a single sprint.

Value management
Value management is a technique to measure the progress on the basis of the delivered value.
