What is Agile Scrum?

 

Scrum is a management framework which helps you deal with complex work. By learning through experiences Scrum encourages teams to work together to get the best result possible. Scrum can be applied to any project or product.

The Scrum framework helps you to expect and adapt more quickly and execute your project in the most effective and efficient way. Scrum uses feedback loops to encourage you to try out, reflect and adapt the product you are working on, and ultimately deliver the product of the highest possible value. Scrum works with sprints. The length of a sprint is usually between two and four weeks. After every sprint a product or a part of a product will be delivered, and the team takes a moment to evaluate the process. After the evaluation a new sprint starts. This process will continue until the project is finished. More information about sprints can be found later on.

Within the Scrum Framework there are three different roles: the Scrum Master, the Product Owner and the Scrum Team. The Scrum Master has a leadership role but has no authority over the Scrum Team. The Scrum Master is the person who facilitates the Scrum process and creates a conducive environment for the Scrum Team. It is the task of the Scrum Master to keep the Scrum Team focused on the project and terminate any distractions.

The Scrum Team consists out of a cross-functional, self-organizing team of about seven people. The Team delivers all the work for the customer. Each Scrum Team consist out of a group of individuals who are dedicated to deliver a product of the highest quality.

The Product Owner is responsible for the success of the project. The Product Owner focusses on the wants and needs of the user and the business goals. It is up to the Product Owner to guide the project in the correct direction. The Product Owner has a leadership role and has to make important decisions during the process, always keeping in mind the vision of the project.
